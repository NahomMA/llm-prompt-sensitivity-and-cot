In this homework I explored how different prompting strategies affect model behavior on sentiment classification and arithmetic reasoning. The main takeaway from my experiments is that small changes to a prompt can produce surprisingly different results. My few shot setup reached 90\% on Gemini but the same prompt scored lower on Claude, and removing a single constraint dropped Claude to 70\% while giving it fewer examples actually pushed it up to 90\%. Chain of thought helped Claude's precision on math problems but hurt Gemini's overall accuracy by introducing errors that cascaded through the reasoning steps. What I found most interesting is that no single prompting approach was consistently better across both models and both tasks.
