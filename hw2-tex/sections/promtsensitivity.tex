\paragraph{(a) Prompt chosen.}
We chose the Problem~1 few-shot sentiment prompt as the baseline since it had strong performance and a clear structure for controlled perturbations. It uses five labeled examples with binary Positive/Negative classification on the same 10 reviews.

\paragraph{(b) Controlled perturbations.}
We produced eight perturbed versions: (1) {reorder}, positive examples first then negative; (2) {rephrase}, (3) {add constraint}, appending 
``Do not explain; output only the label''; (4) {remove constraint}, dropping exactly one word; (5) {fewer examples}, three examples instead of five;
 (6) {contradictory}, one example deliberately mislabeled; (7) {role change}, system role set to 
 product analyst instead of sentiment classifier; (8) {add neutral}, allowing a third Neutral label while gold remains binary.
  All variants are in Appendix~\ref{app:sensitivity}.

\paragraph{(c) Running and recording.}
We ran each variant on the same 10 reviews (Appendix~\ref{app:sentiment-data}) with both models and recorded the predicted label for every combination.

\paragraph{(d) Robustness across models.}
Table~\ref{tab:sensitivity} reports per-variant accuracy for Gemini~2.5 Flash and Claude Haiku.

\paragraph{(e) Sensitivity patterns.}
Gemini was more robust overall, holding at 0.90 for four variants and dropping to 0.80 for the remaining four. Claude was more volatile: removing the output constraint dropped it to 0.70, the lowest score across all conditions, while fewer examples raised it to 0.90, the only case where a perturbation improved over its baseline. The perturbations that caused the largest swings were constraint removal, which hurt both models, and label-space expansion (add neutral) and contradictory labels, which hurt Gemini but left Claude unchanged. Reordering examples had no effect on either model.
